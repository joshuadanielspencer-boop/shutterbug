## Do this first вЂ” it affects every playtester

### A. Stale build served to returning players
> "Every time I start the game the subtitles are always still the transparent blue
> until I force a reload."

`vite.config.js` already sets `registerType: "autoUpdate"`, `clientsClaim`, and
`skipWaiting` вЂ” so the new service worker installs and takes control, but the
**already-open page keeps the assets it loaded with**. The new build only appears
on the next load. That's why a hard reload always "fixes" it.

**Fix:** listen for `controllerchange` (or vite-plugin-pwa's `onNeedRefresh`) and
reload once, automatically, when a new SW takes over. Small change, high value вЂ”
right now every other playtester is potentially reporting bugs against a build
that's days old. **Size: S.**

## Maps

### B. Country maps are zoomed too far out
> "India, Italy, Turkey, China need cropping/zooming significantlyвЂ¦ audit all the
> country mapsвЂ¦ it barely gives the player any perspective they don't have on the
> continent map."

Confirmed as a systemic thing, not four one-offs: the country view fits the
country's bounding box with a flat `VB_PAD = 0.1` margin
(`src/shutterbug-world.jsx:4594`). A country whose bounding box is dominated by
outlying territory (India with the Andamans, Italy with Lampedusa, China with its
western reach) gets framed to the *box*, not to where the country reads.

**Proposed:** audit all 106 countries by computing, for each, the fraction of the
rendered frame the country's own polygon actually fills; anything under a
threshold gets a per-country crop override in data (rule 1 вЂ” a data map, not
inline). I can generate the numbers mechanically and bring you a ranked list
before changing any framing. **Size: M for the audit, MвЂ“L for the overrides.**

### E. "Solomon Is." should read "Solomon Islands"
The abbreviation is the **Natural Earth key**, used in `countries.js`,
`travel.js`, `worldmap.js`, `worldmap-robinson.js`, `culture.js` and
`locations.js`. Do **not** rename the key вЂ” add a display-name override and render
through it, the same way the generated geography file separates data from display
name. **Size: S.** (Worth grepping for other NE abbreviations at the same time вЂ”
there are likely more.)

### G. Distance legend on country maps вЂ” *you asked how hard*
Not crazy: **moderate, and worth doing.** The maps are already projected with
known plate coordinates, so a scale bar is real arithmetic, not a fudge вЂ” pick a
round distance, convert to plate units at the map's centre latitude, draw the bar.
Two honest caveats: (1) on an equirectangular plate the scale changes with
latitude, so the bar is only exactly right across the middle of the frame вЂ” fine
for a big country, misleading for Canada or Russia unless the bar is drawn at the
centre latitude and labelled as such; (2) rule 3 means **miles first, km in
parentheses**. **Size: M.**

### H. Fly to the exact clicked point вЂ” *you asked how hard*
**Easy, and I think you're right that it's better.** `launchFlight(from, to, вЂ¦)`
already takes arbitrary coordinates; the destination is currently snapped to the
target city. Passing the click's own map coordinates instead is a small change.
The nice property you identified is real: landing where they actually pointed
rewards knowing roughly where the country is, without punishing a miss. Applies to
free-choice modes, not hub-based ones. **Size: S.** Keyboard equivalent needs a
thought вЂ” a keyboard user selects a pin, not a point, so they'd keep the snapped
behaviour.
